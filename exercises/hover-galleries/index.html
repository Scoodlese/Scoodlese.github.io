Black-bellied Whistling-Duck

The Black-bellied Whistling-Duck is a boisterous duck with a brilliant pink bill and an unusual, long-legged silhouette. In places like Texas and Louisiana, watch for noisy flocks of these gaudy ducks dropping into fields to forage on seeds, or loafing on golf course ponds. Listen for them, these ducks really do have a whistle for their call.

Canvasback Duck

Duck with distinctive triangular head; forehead slopes seamlessly into the long bill. Males are white-bodied with black chest, reddish head, and red eye. Females are a dull brown with unique head profile and dark brown eye. Spends winters in any large body of water with submerged aquatic. The canvasback dives frequently, searching for vegetation and invertebrates.

Crested Miniature Duck

The Crested is a breed of domestic duck, characterised by a crest or tuft of feathers on the head. These are mostly kept for pets. Crested ducks have been around for a long time and feature in art dating back over 2,000 years.

Marbled duck

Small, subtly beautiful, gray-brown duck with a black eye-mask, long neck and wings, and dark bill. Vaguely resembles a female pintail. Both sexes are similar but males sport a more prominent shaggy crest. In flight looks pallid, with whitish underwings, pale secondaries, and dark wingtips. Mostly silent. Typically found in shallow, freshwater or brackish wetlands with abundant emergent vegetation.
